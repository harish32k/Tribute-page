Sir Arthur Cotton

The delta architect

General Sir Arthur Thomas Cotton KCSI (15 May 1803 â€“ 24 July 1899) was a British general and irrigation engineer.
Cotton devoted his life to the construction of irrigation and navigation canals throughout British India.

Here's a timeline of Sir Arthur Cotton

  • 1803 - Cotton was born
  • 1820 - started his career with the Ordnance Survey at Bangor, North Wales
  • 1821 - appointed for service in India
  • 1828 - promoted to the rank of Captain and was put in charge of Investigation for the Cauveri Scheme
  • 1844 - Cotton recommended the construction of an "anicut"
  • 1848 - proceeded to Australia due to ill health and handed over the charge to Captain Orr
  • 1850 - returned to India and was promoted to the rank of colonel
  • 1852 - succeeded in completing the magnificent project on the Godavari river at Dowleswaram
  • 1855 - Completed construction of aqueduct for Krishna river
  • 1877 - His work in India was much appreciated and he was honoured with KCSI (Knight Commander of the Order of the Star of India)
